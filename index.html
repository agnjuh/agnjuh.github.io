About

My research focuses on how genetic, epigenetic, and environmental cues shape cellular identity, tissue organisation, and regenerative potential. I am interested in the regulatory principles that maintain developmental robustness, coordinate dynamic cellular responses, and preserve genome integrity across contexts.

By connecting molecular mechanisms with higher-order tissue behaviour, I aim to understand how biological systems achieve stability and adaptability, and how their disruption contributes to variation, repair outcomes, and disease.

Experimental and computational expertise

  • CRISPR-based genome editing, stem cell and organoid systems, advanced microscopy
  • Spatial transcriptomics and quantitative imaging of cellular and tissue architecture
  • RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, ChIP-seq, variant analysis, and integrative multi-omics
  • GWAS and gene–environment interaction modelling
  • Machine learning for feature extraction, classification, and biological prediction
  • Reproducible, scalable workflows using Snakemake, Conda, and containerised environments
